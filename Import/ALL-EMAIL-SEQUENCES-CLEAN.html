Subject: Why you've stopped growing (and how to fix it)
Preview text: Progressive overload explained properly

Most lads plateau because they do the same thing every week. Same weight, same reps, same exercises. Their body adapted months ago. It's got no reason to grow.

Progressive overload is the fix. It means giving your body a slightly bigger challenge each session. Not a massive jump. Just enough that your muscles have to adapt.

Three ways to do it:

Add weight. Even 1.25kg per side each week adds up. Over 12 weeks that's 15kg on your bench. That's real progress.

Add reps. If you hit 3x8 last week, aim for 3x9 this week. When you hit 3x12, bump the weight up and drop back to 3x8.

Add sets. Go from 3 sets to 4. More volume, more growth stimulus.

Pick one method per exercise per week. Write everything down. If you're not tracking, you're guessing. And guessing is why most people look the same year after year.

Email 2 (Day 1)

Subject: How GLP-1s actually work (no medical jargon)
Preview text: The science in plain English

GLP-1 stands for glucagon-like peptide 1. That's not important. What matters is what it does.

GLP-1 is a hormone your body makes naturally. When you eat, your gut releases it. It tells your brain "we're full." It slows down digestion. It makes you feel satisfied on less food.

Some people's bodies don't make enough GLP-1. Or their bodies don't respond to it properly. They feel hungry even when they've eaten. They overeat. They gain weight.

GLP-1 medications (Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro) are synthetic versions of this hormone. You inject them once a week. They tell your brain you're full. You eat less. You lose weight. That's it.

They also slow digestion and reduce cravings. They can improve blood sugar control if you have diabetes. They can reduce your appetite for junk food specifically.

The side effects come from slowing your digestion too much. Nausea, constipation, vomiting. These usually ease after a few weeks as your body adjusts.

They're not magic. They're a tool. If you eat 5,000 calories daily, a GLP-1 won't fix that overnight. But if you've struggled with hunger and appetite control, they actually work.

Email 5 (Day 7)

Subject: Side effects: what research says vs what TikTok says
Preview text: Evidence-based breakdown

TikTok calls GLP-1 side effects "ozempic face" and rapid muscle loss. The research says something different (though not completely different).

Nausea and vomiting. Up to 40% of people experience this in the first few weeks. Usually mild. Usually stops within 2-3 weeks. Drinking water and eating small meals helps.

Constipation. Common. Happens because GLP-1s slow digestion. Fibre, water, and movement fix it. Not serious.

Muscle loss. This is real but preventable. GLP-1s suppress appetite. If you eat too little protein, you lose muscle along with fat. Solution: eat 120-150g protein daily and do strength training. You'll lose fat and keep muscle.

Face changes. Your face can look different when you lose weight quickly. Not because of the drug. Because of the weight loss. If you lose weight slowly, your face adjusts gradually.

Rare stuff. Pancreatitis is extremely rare. Thyroid issues found in mice, not humans. Always reported in clinical trials. Your GP will screen you.

Most side effects are mild, temporary, and manageable. If they're severe, your prescriber will change your dose.

GLP-1s make it easy to eat less. That's their job. But eating less doesn't automatically mean you'll be healthy. You could lose weight and feel terrible.
