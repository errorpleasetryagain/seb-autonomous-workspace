Social Media Setup Checklist

4 accounts. About 10 mins each. Use "Sebastian" everywhere. No personal photos.

RULE: Name = Sebastian or Seb. Never Adam. No personal face photos. Use site logos or stock.
Which approach? You have two options:

Option A: One account per site (7 YouTube channels, 7 Instagrams etc). More work but each looks dedicated to its niche. Better for affiliate applications.

Option B: One umbrella account ("Good Living Co" or "Sebastian"). Less work, one link for all affiliate signups. Easier to manage.

My recommendation: Start with Option B (one account each). You can always spin up niche-specific ones later. Right now you just need something that looks real for affiliate applications.

1. YouTube

You need this anyway for faceless videos later.

1. Go to youtube.com, sign in with Google
2. Create a channel. Name: Sebastian or Good Living Co
3. Add a profile picture (site logo or a simple icon)
4. Add this bio:
Evidence-based health, fitness, and productivity content for men who want to do things properly. No hype, no fluff. UK-based. New videos weekly. Sites: maleoptimal.co.uk | liftlab.co.uk | fueloptimal.co.uk | aibyrole.co.uk | tradepick.co.uk | remotepivot.co.uk | glp1guide.co.uk
5. Add your website link: maleoptimal.co.uk (your primary site)
6. Done. Copy the channel URL for affiliate signups.

2. Instagram

1. Download Instagram app or go to instagram.com
2. Sign up. Username: goodlivingco_ or sebastianwrites_ (check what's free)
3. Switch to a Business account (Settings > Account > Switch to Professional)
4. Category: Health/Beauty or Education
5. Profile pic: same as YouTube
6. Bio:
Evidence-based health + productivity for men 40+ UK content. No bro science. Hormone optimisation | Fitness | AI tools maleoptimal.co.uk
7. Done. Copy the profile URL for affiliate signups.

3. X (Twitter)

1. Go to x.com, sign up
2. Display name: Sebastian. Handle: @goodlivingco or @sebwrites (check what's free)
3. Profile pic: same as others
4. Bio:
Evidence-based health, fitness + productivity for men 40+. UK. No hype. maleoptimal.co.uk
5. Pin a tweet: "Building something. Evidence-based health and productivity content for men who want straight answers. More soon."
6. Done. Copy the profile URL for affiliate signups.

4. LinkedIn

Two options here:

Option A: Use your existing personal LinkedIn. You don't need to add "Sebastian" here because LinkedIn is your professional profile. Affiliate programmes just want to see a real person. This is the easiest option.
Option B: Create a Company Page for "Good Living Co". Go to linkedin.com/company/setup/new. This looks more professional for affiliate apps but takes more effort.
Either way, copy the URL for affiliate signups.

What to Put on Affiliate Signup Forms

Website URL: maleoptimal.co.uk (or whichever site the programme is for)
YouTube: paste your channel URL
Instagram: paste your profile URL
X/Twitter: paste your profile URL
LinkedIn: paste your personal or company page URL
If a field is optional: leave it blank. Don't stress about filling in every social link.
If a field is required but you haven't set it up yet: put your website URL instead. Most programmes won't reject you for that.

Tip: After creating accounts, post 2-3 things on each (share an article from your site, a health tip, anything). A completely empty account looks worse than one with even a few posts.
