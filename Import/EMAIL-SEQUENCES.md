### Email 2 — Day 1

**Subject:** The one number your GP checks (and why it's not enough)
**Preview text:** Total testosterone without SHBG is almost meaningless

---

Most GP surgeries will check your total testosterone. It's the first thing they look at, and it's also why most men walk away thinking they're fine when they're actually not.

Here's the problem: total testosterone tells you half the story.

Think of it like having cash in your bank account and cash in your pocket. Total testosterone is the total amount. Free testosterone is what's actually usable. The stuff doing the work.

SHBG — sex hormone binding globulin — is the thing binding it up. If your total testosterone looks okay but your SHBG is high (which happens with age, poor sleep, and excess body fat), your free testosterone could be genuinely low. That's when you get symptoms: fatigue, brain fog, weak recovery in the gym, mood starting to slip.

Your GP probably won't test free testosterone or SHBG unless you push them. That's why the checklist matters. It tells you what to ask for.

I've written a full guide on this: [SITE_URL]/guides/free-testosterone-explained

The short version: get tested properly. If you haven't ordered yet, grab the Medichecks panel I recommended yesterday. It tests total T, free T, SHBG, and a few other useful markers. [MEDICHECKS_LINK]
