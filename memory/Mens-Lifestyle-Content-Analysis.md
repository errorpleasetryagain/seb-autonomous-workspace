# Men's Lifestyle Content Analysis
## GQ vs Men's Health — Finding the Gap

### GQ UK Positioning

**Tone:** Aspirational, luxury-adjacent
**Language:** "Hench bros," "swoll bros," "swole"
**Content:**
- Celebrity/athlete meal features
- High-end ingredients
- "Slopocalypse" commentary
- Fashion-adjacent wellness
**Price Point:** Premium (£££)
**Accessibility:** Low — requires disposable income

**Typical Headlines:**
- "11 high protein meals for max gains"
- "The best foods for bulking if anyone fancies getting really swole"
- "How to eat right in 2026, according to some hench bros"

**Approach:**
- Feature-driven (celebrity chefs, athletes)
- Expensive ingredients (wagyu, truffles)
- Restaurant-quality at home
- Status signaling through food

---

### Men's Health UK Positioning

**Tone:** Scientific, instructional
**Language:** Clinical, precise
**Content:**
- Macro breakdowns
- Evidence-based nutrition
- Supplement deep-dives
- Workout-aligned eating
**Price Point:** Mid-range (££)
**Accessibility:** Medium — requires knowledge

**Typical Headlines:**
- "The Strengthspan Diet – How to Eat to Stay Strong"
- "How to Bulk Without Packing on Unnecessary Fat"
- "This Cheap Breakfast Staple May Boost Muscle Power"

**Approach:**
- Education-heavy
- Trend analysis ("dense bean salad craze")
- One-pan efficiency
- Supplement integration

### Voice and Tone Guide

**Do:**
- Straight-talking, no fluff
- Specific numbers (£1.80, 42g protein, 18 minutes)
- Respect reader's time and money
- "Here's how" not "Imagine if"
- Acknowledge real constraints (budget, time, skill)

**Don't:**
- Aspirational lifestyle content
- Celebrity name-dropping
- Expensive ingredient requirements
- "Invest in yourself" messaging
- Gym bro clichés ("smash," "destroy," "beast mode")

**Sample Voice:**

GQ: "Fuel your gains with this athlete-approved macro bowl featuring sustainably sourced salmon and ancient grains."

MH: "This meal delivers 38g of complete protein with a favourable omega-3 to omega-6 ratio."

**FO:** "Salmon's expensive. This tinned tuna version gives you 40g protein for £1.50. Tastes better than it sounds."
